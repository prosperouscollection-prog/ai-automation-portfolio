# Discovery Call Questionnaire

Use this document before, during, and immediately after every client discovery call.

The goal is simple: understand the business problem, qualify the prospect, and leave the call with a clear next step.

## Pre-Call Research

Before the call, spend 10 to 15 minutes gathering context.

### Review Their Website

- What services do they offer?
- Is the offer clear?
- Do they have a visible contact form?
- Do they have online booking?
- Do they have a chatbot?
- Is the website modern or outdated?
- Are there obvious missed-conversion points?

### Review Their Social Media Presence

- Are they active on Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, or TikTok?
- Are they posting consistently?
- Is video content present?
- Are comments or DMs likely part of their lead flow?
- Do they appear understaffed on content output?

### Review Their Google Reviews

- What do customers praise most?
- What do customers complain about?
- Are there complaints about response time, phone issues, scheduling, or communication?
- What language can you mirror on the call?

### Review Their Current Tech Stack If Visible

- Do they use online forms?
- Do they use scheduling tools?
- Do they use chat widgets?
- Do they use a CRM?
- Is there evidence of automation, email flows, or online booking software?

### Identify Likely Pain Points By Industry

- Dental: missed calls, repetitive insurance questions, appointment scheduling, after-hours emergencies
- Salon / med spa: DMs, booking questions, no-show reminders, front-desk overload
- HVAC / home services: speed to lead, after-hours inquiries, dispatch coordination
- Legal / professional services: lead qualification, intake volume, repetitive FAQ handling

## Call Opening

### Exact Opening Words

> Thanks for taking the time today. My goal for this call is to understand how your business currently handles [leads / calls / customer questions / content], where the bottlenecks are, and whether there’s a practical AI automation solution that actually makes sense for you. If there is, I’ll recommend the simplest starting point. If there isn’t, I’ll tell you that too.

### How To Set The Agenda

> I’d love to spend the first few minutes understanding your current process, then I’ll ask a few questions about pain points, goals, and timing. From there, I can tell you what I’d recommend and what the next step would be. Sound good?

### How To Establish Rapport Quickly

- Mention one observation from their website, reviews, or industry
- Keep it natural, not overdone

Example:

> I noticed you have a lot of strong reviews around friendly service, which usually means your front-desk or client experience matters a lot. That’s helpful context for this conversation.

## Discovery Questions

Use 10 to 15 of these depending on the flow of the call.

### Current Process

1. How are you currently handling [leads / calls / support questions / content creation] today?
2. What happens after hours or when the team gets busy?
3. Where do inquiries usually come in from: phone, website, social media, email, or somewhere else?
4. Who on your team currently owns this process?

### Pain Points

5. What part of this process is the biggest headache right now?
6. Where do you think opportunities are falling through the cracks?
7. Are there repetitive questions or tasks your team handles over and over?
8. If you could remove one bottleneck immediately, what would it be?

### Impact And Priority

9. What is this issue costing you right now in time, missed leads, or staff frustration?
10. How urgent is this for you on a scale from 1 to 10?
11. If nothing changes over the next 3 months, what happens?

### Tech Comfort And Implementation

12. How comfortable is your team with using new tools if I make them simple?
13. Do you already use tools like Gmail, Google Sheets, CRMs, booking software, or chat widgets?
14. Do you prefer something very lightweight, or are you open to a more customized setup?

### Budget And Decision Process

15. So I can recommend something realistic, are you thinking about this as a small starter project, or are you open to investing in a more complete solution if the ROI is clear?

If they need a more direct budget question:

> Is this something you’re hoping to keep under $1,000, in the $1,000 to $3,000 range, or above that if the business case makes sense?

16. Besides you, who else would be involved in approving a project like this?
17. What does your decision process usually look like for something like this?

### Timeline And Success

18. If we moved forward, what timeline would feel ideal?
19. What would success look like 30 days after this goes live?
20. If this worked exactly the way you want, what would improve first?

## Qualifying Criteria

### Green Flags

- They clearly feel the pain
- They can describe the current process
- They understand the business impact
- They ask practical questions
- They have authority or direct access to the decision-maker
- They are open to change
- They respond quickly and professionally
- They are not shopping only on lowest price

### Red Flags

- They want free consulting but avoid commitment
- They are vague about the problem
- They want enterprise complexity on a tiny budget
- They resist deposits
- They have no urgency and no real pain
- They speak badly about every past contractor
- They want you to “just figure it all out” with no input
- They expect unlimited work without boundaries

### Prospect Scoring

#### Hot

- clear problem
- clear urgency
- authority present
- budget likely available
- willing to take next step quickly

#### Warm

- real problem
- moderate urgency
- needs education
- may need internal approval
- interested but not ready yet

#### Cold

- curiosity only
- vague pain
- low urgency
- weak fit
- price-shopping behavior
- no commitment to next step

## Call Closing

### Exact Words To Close

> This has been really helpful. Based on what you’ve shared, I think the main opportunity is [summarize problem], and the most practical starting point would be [recommended project / approach].

### How To Propose Next Steps

> The next step on my side would be to send you a short proposal that outlines the recommended setup, timeline, pricing, and what’s included. If that looks good, we’d move into contract, deposit, and kickoff.

### How To Get A Commitment Before Hanging Up

> Does that sound like the right next step? If so, I’ll send that over by [specific time today], and if you’d like, we can tentatively hold [day] for a kickoff.

If they are unsure:

> Totally fine. What would you need to see in the proposal to make a confident decision?

## Post-Call Actions

### What To Do Within 1 Hour

1. Log the call notes in your CRM
2. Score the prospect as Hot, Warm, or Cold
3. Record the top pain points and buying triggers
4. Send the same-day follow-up email
5. If qualified, send the proposal within 2 hours
6. Add a follow-up reminder to your calendar or CRM

### Same-Day Follow Up Email Template

**Subject:** Great speaking with you today

Hi [First Name],

Thanks again for taking the time to speak today.

Based on our conversation, the main opportunity I see is [brief summary of problem], and I believe the strongest starting point is [recommended solution].

As discussed, I’ll send over [the proposal / next steps / demo / summary] by [time or date].

If anything comes to mind in the meantime, feel free to reply here.

Best,  
[Your Name]

### How To Log The Prospect In Your CRM

Track at minimum:

- Date of call
- Prospect name
- Business name
- Industry
- Contact info
- Main pain point
- Recommended project
- Budget notes
- Decision-maker
- Timeline
- Hot / Warm / Cold score
- Next action
- Follow-up date

## Simple Rule For Every Discovery Call

Do not leave the call without:

- understanding the real problem
- identifying the likely best-fit solution
- knowing who decides
- knowing whether budget is plausible
- getting agreement on the next step
