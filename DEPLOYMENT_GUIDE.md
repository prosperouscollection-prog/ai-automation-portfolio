## 2. Client Onboarding Process

This is the path from cold prospect to recurring retainer.

### Step 1: First Message Sent

- Goal: start a conversation, not sell everything
- Tool: email, LinkedIn DM, or local business contact form
- Output: short message with one pain point and one relevant demo

### Step 2: They Respond

- Goal: qualify interest quickly
- Ask:
  - What system are you currently using?
  - What happens today when a lead comes in after hours?
  - What is that costing you?
- Output: short qualification notes in your CRM

### Step 3: Demo Call Booked

- Tool: Calendly
- Goal: get them on a live 15-minute call
- Rule: never do unpaid custom strategy before the demo call

### Step 4: Demo Delivered

- Goal: show the before/after outcome
- Structure:
  - problem
  - live demo
  - business impact
  - next step
- Output: verbal interest or objection list

### Step 5: Proposal Sent

- Send within 2 hours after the call
- Include:
  - problem summary
  - proposed solution
  - deliverables
  - timeline
  - setup fee
  - monthly retainer
  - next step

### Step 6: Contract Signed

- Send contract only after verbal alignment on scope and price
- Require signatures before any implementation work begins

### Step 7: Invoice Sent

- Always invoice 50% upfront on setup
- Retainer begins only after setup goes live

### Step 8: Payment Received

- No work starts until deposit clears
- Mark client as `active` in your CRM

### Step 9: Project Kicked Off

- Collect:
  - logins
  - business info
  - FAQs
  - scripts
  - services
  - hours
  - branding assets
- Set a delivery date

### Step 10: Project Delivered

- Run final QA
- Deliver live walkthrough
- Get written approval

### Step 11: Retainer Started

- Monthly retainer covers:
  - monitoring
  - bug fixes
  - light updates
  - prompt tuning
  - reporting
- Set the recurring invoice immediately after go-live

## 3. Demo Script

Use this for a 10-minute live call. Speak calmly and keep momentum. Do not over-explain the tech unless they ask.

### Minute 0 to 1: Opening

> Thanks for taking the call. What I want to do in the next 10 minutes is show you 7 AI systems I’ve built for local businesses, explain which ones actually matter for your business, and show you how they can save time, capture more leads, and improve response speed without adding headcount.

### Minute 1 to 2: Project 1

> The first system is Lead Capture plus AI Scoring. A lead comes in through a form or webhook, AI reads the message, scores it as high, medium, or low intent, and logs it to Google Sheets so your team knows who to call first. This is useful if leads are coming in but follow-up is inconsistent.

### Transition

> Once you know which leads matter most, the next question is how fast you respond.

### Minute 2 to 3: Project 4

> That’s where the Workflow Automation plus Email system comes in. A lead is captured, logged, scored, and automatically emailed within seconds. That improves speed to lead, which is often the difference between winning and losing the customer.

### Transition

> Some businesses also lose leads before a form is ever filled out, especially by phone.

### Minute 3 to 4: Project 2

> The AI Voice Agent answers inbound calls 24/7. If someone calls after hours and says, “I have a toothache,” the assistant can respond immediately, collect their information, and move them toward booking. That means fewer missed opportunities and better call coverage without hiring another front-desk person.

### Transition

> The same principle applies on your website too.

### Minute 4 to 5: Project 5

> The AI Customer Service Chat Widget sits on a website and answers common questions instantly. Hours, services, insurance, booking questions, all of that can be handled automatically so visitors don’t leave before getting what they need.

### Transition

> To make that kind of customer support safer and more accurate, we can ground it in your actual business information.

### Minute 5 to 6: Project 3

> The RAG Knowledge Base Chatbot uses your own FAQ or support document. So instead of guessing, it answers from your actual policies and escalates anything unknown professionally. This is how you reduce repetitive staff questions without increasing risk.

### Transition

> If you want the AI to sound even more like your business, there’s a deeper layer.

### Minute 6 to 7: Project 6

> The Fine-Tuned AI Model is for businesses that want the assistant to sound exactly like them. Same warmth, same phrasing, same tone. That matters if brand voice and customer trust are part of the experience you sell.

### Transition

> The final system is less about support and more about growth.

### Minute 7 to 8: Project 7

> The AI Video Automation Pipeline takes one topic, like “5 reasons to choose Invisalign,” and turns it into a full short-form content package: script, captions, hashtags, and thumbnail text. It is useful for businesses that know they need content but never have time to produce it consistently.

### Minute 8 to 9: Tie It Back to Their Business

> The important part is that you would not need all 7 of these. My job is to identify which one or two solve the biggest bottleneck in your business first. Usually that’s missed leads, slow response time, or repetitive front-desk workload.

### Minute 9 to 10: Close

> Based on what you told me, I’d recommend starting with [best-fit system]. If you want, my next step is simple: I’ll send you a short proposal with the setup fee, monthly support retainer, delivery timeline, and exactly what I’d build for your business. If it looks good, we can get started with a 50% deposit and move quickly.

### How To Handle Price Objections

If they say `That’s expensive`:

> I understand. The real question is what the current problem is costing you every month. If this helps you recover even a few missed leads or saves staff time every week, it usually pays for itself quickly.

If they say `Can you do it cheaper?`:

> I can reduce scope, but I do not reduce price without reducing deliverables. We can start with a smaller version and expand once it proves ROI.

If they say `We need to think about it`:

> Completely fair. I’ll send a clear proposal today so you can review it internally. I’ll follow up on [specific date].

### When To Offer A Discount

Offer a discount only when:

- they are an ideal niche client you want as a flagship case study
- they move fast and pay without friction
- they agree to a testimonial or case study
- the discount is tied to reduced scope or a longer retainer commitment

### When To Hold Firm

Hold firm when:

- they ask for custom work beyond the agreed scope
- they compare your service to cheap overseas labor
- they want strategy, setup, and support for one low fee
- they hesitate on a reasonable setup fee but expect high urgency
- they already show signs of scope creep

## 5. Contract Essentials

Every client contract should include:

- Legal names of both parties
- Clear description of the project
- Exact deliverables
- What is not included
- Client responsibilities for access, approvals, and content
- Timeline and milestone dates
- Payment terms
- Revision policy
- Support terms
- Data/privacy language
- Limitation of liability
- Cancellation terms
- Governing law

### Payment Terms

- 50% upfront always
- 50% due before final handoff or go-live
- Monthly retainer billed in advance
- Late fee begins after 7 days if unpaid
- Work pauses automatically if invoices are overdue

### Scope Boundaries

Your contract must clearly state:

- number of workflows or systems included
- number of integrations included
- number of revisions included
- whether copywriting, design, hosting, and tool subscriptions are included
- whether staff training is included

### Revision Policy

- 1 to 2 rounds of revisions on initial build
- revision requests must be submitted within 7 days of delivery
- anything beyond scope becomes a paid change request

### Cancellation Policy For Retainers

- 30 days written notice
- no partial-month refunds
- access or licenses managed by you may be disabled at the end of the notice period
- emergency support ends immediately when the retainer ends unless separately contracted

### Important Note

This guide is business guidance, not legal advice. Use a lawyer-reviewed template before sending real contracts.

- Small fixed tools under $25/month used across your whole business: bury them in your retainer

If the tool cost scales with their usage, the client should pay for it.

## 7. Common Client Questions And Answers

### How does this work?

> We connect your existing lead, phone, or content process to an AI workflow. The AI handles the repetitive first layer, logs what happens, and either completes the task or escalates it to a human.

### Is my data secure?

> I design these systems to minimize unnecessary data exposure, use reputable vendors, and keep access limited. For sensitive or regulated data, I also define exactly what should and should not be sent through AI services.

### What if the AI makes a mistake?

> We design for human oversight, fallback rules, and safe escalation. The goal is not zero oversight forever. The goal is handling the repetitive 80% well and routing the exceptions safely.

### Can I cancel anytime?

> You can cancel the monthly retainer with written notice according to the agreement. Build fees already paid are non-refundable once the work has been completed.

### Do I need technical knowledge to use this?

> No. I handle setup and implementation. Your team only needs a simple operating process, which I document and walk through with you.

### What happens if something breaks?

> That is exactly what the maintenance retainer is for. I monitor the system, fix issues, update prompts or workflows, and keep things running.

### How is this different from hiring a person?

> A person brings judgment and relationship skills. AI brings 24/7 availability, instant response time, and low-cost handling of repetitive work. The best setup is usually AI for first-pass work and people for exceptions and high-value conversations.

### Can you customize it for my specific business?

> Yes. That is the point. The demos show the system type. The production version is customized around your services, hours, policies, tone, workflows, and team process.

## 8. Red Flags

### Signs Of A Bad Client

- They want a lot of free strategy before paying
- They are vague about what they want but very certain about what they want to pay
- They expect instant replies at all hours before becoming a client
- They resist deposits
- They ask for “just one more thing” repeatedly before signing
- They talk badly about every past freelancer
- They want you to copy a competitor exactly
- They dismiss security, approvals, or documentation as unnecessary

### Scope Creep Warning Signs

- “Can you also add this since it should be easy?”
- “While you’re in there…”
- “We assumed training was included”
- “Can you also build a dashboard?”
- “Can we just add SMS, call routing, and booking too?”

### When To Fire A Client

Fire a client when:

- invoices are repeatedly late
- they become abusive or disrespectful
- they demand work outside contract repeatedly
- they ask you to hide compliance or privacy issues
- they refuse to give needed access but still blame you for delays
- the account becomes unprofitable and they reject scope correction

## 10. Emergency Troubleshooting

### If n8n Goes Down

Do this:

1. Check whether the issue is hosting, credentials, rate limits, or a broken node
2. Pause any dependent live automations if needed
3. Run the key workflow manually if the client has urgent leads waiting
4. Restore from backup or redeploy if the instance is corrupted
5. Document the incident and the fix

Client message:

> We identified an automation issue affecting the workflow. I’ve isolated the problem, I’m actively fixing it, and I’ll keep you updated until service is fully restored. If any urgent leads come in during this window, I’ll help make sure they are handled manually.

### If OpenAI API Fails

Do this:

1. Check OpenAI status and your usage dashboard
2. Confirm API key validity and billing status
3. Retry with lower load or fallback messaging
4. Switch to a safe fallback response if needed
5. Log all failures for review

Client message:

> The issue is currently upstream with the AI provider. I’ve put a fallback in place so users still get a safe response while I monitor restoration and test normal service.

### If Google Sheets Stops Updating

Do this:

1. Check Google auth token validity
2. Confirm the sheet still exists and headers are unchanged
3. Test a manual append
4. Queue failed items temporarily in n8n or local logs
5. Backfill missed rows once access is restored

Client message:

> The logging layer stopped writing to the sheet, but the core system is still being checked. I’m restoring the connection and will backfill any missed records so nothing is lost.

### If Vapi Agent Stops Working

Do this:

1. Confirm the phone number route and Vapi account status
2. Check connected model, voice, and telephony provider settings
3. Test inbound and fallback call behavior
4. Route calls to a human backup number if needed
5. Review logs and concurrency limits

Client message:

> I’m seeing an issue with the voice agent routing. I’m testing the full call path now and will either restore the agent or temporarily forward calls to a human fallback so leads are not missed.

### Professional Communication Rules During Issues

- Acknowledge quickly
- State what is affected
- State what is not affected
- Explain what you are doing now
- Give the next update time
- Never blame the client
- Never disappear during an outage
